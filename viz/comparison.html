Verdict

Neural Hawkes (CT-LSTM) beats the classical exponential Hawkes baseline on held-out log-likelihood by +0.64 nats per event (−4.856 vs −5.497). The improvement is statistically meaningful (neural std: ±0.004) and suggests that nonlinear, history-dependent intensity dynamics — which the CT-LSTM can capture but the exponential kernel cannot — are present in this Polymarket weather data. However, the classical model's high branching ratio (0.94) already indicates strong cross-excitation among wallets, and its sparse influence matrix provides interpretable structure that the neural model lacks. For a production signal, the classical model's interpretable influence pairs are more actionable; the neural model's improvement confirms the signal is real but doesn't yet reveal which wallet relationships drive it. Next steps: (1) extract attention-based influence from the neural model, (2) test on additional weather markets beyond Seoul, (3) evaluate whether the top classical influence pairs predict out-of-sample trading patterns.
